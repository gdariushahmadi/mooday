Peer-to-Peer Fashion Marketplace

Your wardrobe has more to give.

Mooday is a social marketplace built for the UAE where women resell — or rent out — the fashion items they've bought or received as gifts. Dresses, shoes, bags, and accessories find a second life with someone new, at a price that makes sense. Designed mobile-first, with a fully native Arabic & English experience.

— 01 / Concept

From your closet to someone else's story.

Every woman has a wardrobe full of pieces she wore once, never wore, or simply outgrew — a dress from a wedding, a handbag that doesn't match her style anymore, a gift she can't use. Mooday is a peer-to-peer marketplace where she can resell those items — or rent them out for a special occasion — and discover pre-loved fashion from other women in the UAE along the way.

— 02 / Features

Built for both sides of the deal.

Every screen below is a live iframe of the running application — for the woman selling or renting out a piece she no longer wears, and for the one who will love it next. The interactive prototypes respond to clicks, navigation, and the in-app language switcher.

01 · Discovery

A feed of pre-loved pieces, hand-picked for you

The home feed blends "For You", "Trending" and "Designers" lanes with category chips and two view modes (Featured / Compact). Each card previews the original retail price alongside the asking price, so buyers can see the saving at a glance and sellers can highlight the value they're offering.

  • Tabbed lanes: For You · Trending · Designers
  • Category chips: Bags · Clothing · Home · Lifestyle
  • Featured & compact grid view toggle
  • Inline like / save with animated heart — build your "Loves"
  • Original price vs. asking price — transparency built-in
  • Live currency formatting in AED

02 · Search

Smart search & advanced filters

A dedicated search experience with a full-text input, category and condition filters, and instant filtering. The mock catalogue covers four categories and three condition tiers to demonstrate the filter engine.

  • Real-time text search across titles & descriptions
  • Category & condition multi-select
  • Empty-state and active-filter UI states
  • Direct jump from a result to product details

Rich detail pages that build trust

A full-bleed media gallery, condition badges (New with Tags, Excellent, Gently Used), and the seller's mini-profile with her own rating. Three clear CTAs let a buyer "Chat with Owner", "Add to Bag" or "Buy Now" — whatever feels right for the moment.

  • Image gallery with multiple angles
  • Condition badges: New with Tags · Excellent · Gently Used
  • Owner mini-card with rating & profile
  • Three CTAs: Buy Now · Add to Bag · Chat
  • Original retail price shown for comparison

04 · Sell

List a piece from your closet in minutes

A guided bilingual form for the woman clearing out her wardrobe. She snaps a photo (or picks from a library), fills in the title and description in both English and Arabic, sets her asking price and the original retail price for context, picks a condition, and publishes — her item goes straight into the feed.

  • Photo picker from camera or gallery
  • EN + AR title and description fields
  • Asking price + original retail price (so the discount % is shown automatically)
  • Category and condition selectors
  • Listings persist on the device — the foundation for a real backend later
Live Preview
05 · Checkout

A checkout that feels safe for both sides

A two-step flow tailored for the UAE: delivery address with all seven emirates, then a payment step supporting card and Apple Pay. Shipping is automatically waived for orders above AED 1,000. The buyer's money is held in escrow until she confirms the item arrived as described — protecting both her and the seller.

  • Step 1: Address (name, phone, emirate, street)
  • Step 2: Payment (card or Apple Pay)
  • Live order summary & total calculation
  • Free shipping over AED 1,000
  • Success animation on completion

The "Vault" is the user's personal hub: a profile card with her rating and stats, plus three tabs separating the items she's currently selling, the pieces she's saved as "Loves", and all her conversations and offers in one place.

  • Profile card: name, handle, location, rating
  • Stats: followers, following, reviews
  • My Closet · My Loves · Chats / Offers tabs
  • Re-open any conversation with one tap

The chat overlay includes a smart auto-reply that recognises keywords like "authentic", "offer" and "condition" in both English and Arabic — so a quick question about a dress or a handbag gets a useful answer even before the owner is online.

  • Activity stream: messages, offers, follows, saves

— 03 / Bilingual

Built Arabic-first, not bolted on.

The entire UI ships with English and Arabic translations. Typography swaps to El Messiri and Noto Sans Arabic in RTL, icons are automatically mirrored, and every content field supports both languages — including user-generated listings.

One tap, two worlds.

The language switcher is available in the header of every screen and persists across sessions. Layouts, fonts, icons, and content all adapt instantly.

  • Independent EN + AR titles, descriptions, categories
  • Auto-mirrored icons & layout in RTL
  • Persisted preference in localStorage
  • Chat auto-replies translated per locale
  • Fonts: Bodoni Moda (EN) · El Messiri (AR)

— 06 / Roadmap

What ships next.

The current build covers the full user journey end-to-end on the frontend. The next phases plug in real authentication, payments, and a backend API.

Phase 1 · Now

Frontend prototype (v0.1)

All 9 user-facing views of the buyer journey (Discover, Search, Product, Sell, Checkout, Vault, Activity, Chat, Settings) are wired together in Next.js 16 / React 19 with a single bilingual EN+AR data model. Every listing, cart line, chat and like persists to localStorage so the full loop — from browsing to checkout success animation — runs offline. The Sell form already accepts bilingual title and description, asking price vs. original retail price, category and condition, and queues new items straight into the Discover feed. This is the artifact we use for stakeholder review, design QA and copywriting sign-off.

Phase 2 · Next

Admin panel & content moderation

Build the internal web console the team will use day-to-day before any real users land. A staff-only /admin route in the same Next.js app, gated by a role check on the future auth layer: listings queue with approve / reject / request edits, user management (suspend, verify, refund flag), category & condition dictionaries, translation QA (side-by-side EN/AR string editor), featured-curation for the Discover feed, and basic analytics (DAU, listings/day, conversion). Also defines the contract between admin actions and the future backend, so every moderation event is an audit log entry from day one.

Phase 3

Backend API & database

Stand up the real backend so the prototype stops living in localStorage. NestJS (or Next.js Route Handlers) exposing a versioned REST + WebSocket API, with Postgres + Prisma as the system of record for users, listings, orders, chats and reviews. Auth: email / phone OTP, with UAE Pass as a stretch goal. Image pipeline on S3 + CloudFront with on-the-fly resizing. Algolia (or Meilisearch) for the search index, kept in sync via DB triggers. Replace the useApp() mock layer view-by-view without changing the UI contracts.

Phase 4

Rent mode — listing & booking engine

Introduce the second listing mode alongside Resell. Owners toggle a listing to "Rentable", set a daily rate, a refundable security deposit and a min/max rental window. Buyers pick start and end dates from an availability calendar that respects blocked dates, existing holds and the owner's lead time. Each booking produces a rental contract with pickup / return logistics and a damage-checklist flow on return. Rent pricing, deposits and late fees all flow through the same payment primitives that Phase 5 introduces — so payments and rent land in the right order.

Phase 5

Payments & escrow

Plug a UAE-friendly PSP — Checkout.com or Stripe with AED — behind a single PaymentProvider interface. Card + Apple Pay at checkout, with the shipping-over-AED-1,000 waiver preserved. Buyer funds move into an escrow ledger, released only after the buyer confirms the item arrived as described (or auto-released after N days). Refunds, partial refunds and dispute holds are first-class states. The same engine handles rent: rental fee + deposit held, deposit returned on clean return. Webhooks are the source of truth — no client-side status mutations.

Phase 6

Native apps & push

Wrap the web app with Expo / React Native for iOS and Android so we get a real store presence and platform integrations. Push notifications for new offers, follows, chat messages and order-status changes. Deep links from push straight into the relevant product, chat or order. In-app review prompt on delivery confirmation. Native camera for the Sell flow so listings get better photos than a web file picker.
